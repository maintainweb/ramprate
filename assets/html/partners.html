Here’s the plan.

Standing between consistently landing new business wins and achieving a high customer retention rate are a number of mysteries. Simply stated, it’s not easy to get inside your prospects’ heads. You seldom know where you stand with them, what they perceive to be your strengths and weaknesses, and the moves you can make to close.

Our plan, the RampRate Collaboration Program, helps service providers overcome these hurdles. Created to complement the RampRate Ensemble partner program, the collaboration program provides you answers, with:

REPORTS

In-depth reports give you insights about how your company and services are perceived so you can refine your offerings and sales strategies.

BUSINESS MODEL SOLUTIONS

A trio of business models help us better assess client workload requirements to architect effective solutions and maximize margins.

Close more deals with RampRate reports.

The collaboration program offers six report types to support your sales and customer retention strategies. Data contained in the reports is derived from RampRate’s engagements with clients and service providers in our large supplier ecosystem.

You have the option to order any number of specific reports or subscribe to our reporting service. In advance, RampRate specialists will consult with you to customize the reports to best meet your needs.

Report types include:

EXECUTION REPORTS

Score report— Delivers objective data based on the RampRate Service Provider Intelligence Index to depict where you stand relative to your competition.

Pricer report—Reveals current market pricing across the array of infrastructure services by each company.

Contracts report—Presents a detailed comparison of IT services contract against RampRate’s 200+ best practice contract terms.

STRATEGY REPORTS

Position report—Compares your services vs. competitors across eight categories in a scorecard format.

Planner report—Delivers more than 100 country profiles to help you geo-target to increase conversion.

Operations report —A comprehensive guide to operational practices for global hosting providers that are developing new services.

Grow your margins with RampRate advisory services.

The collaboration program provides advisory services to help enhance your business model to address the growing sophistication of technology buyers.

If you provide cloud, colocation or content delivery network services, RampRate can show you how to deliver leading edge solutions while growing margins.

APPLICATION WORKLOAD SOURCING

A new approach to sourcing, application workload sourcing features a purpose, multiple tiers of importance, performance and resilience requirements. With this business model, the workload is optimized based on consumption, performance, behavior, and economic modeling.

RampRate recommends an pragmatic architecture to run applications to support current and future needs and creates accurate future state cost, risk and performance scores.

COST-OF-COMPUTE SOURCING

RampRate believes a cost-of-compute model delivers a truer economic model. An "all in" cost-of-compute dollar figure is used to reflect true costs. The figure changes based on the infrastructure services consumed in running applications and on the infrastructure costs. 

Suppliers/IT infrastructure operators optimize the costs by maximizing density and utilization rates of capital and human resources. Buyers optimize costs by reducing waste, paying optimally, and matching workload behaviors/demands to the right-fit resource pool.

REAL-TIME INFRASTRUCTURE SOURCING EXCHANGE (RISE)

RampRate’s RISE model shifts the standard buying process to an open marketplace bidding process where you bid on units of compute. To submit your bids, you are given in-depth information about infrastructure service requirements, consumption, behaviors, goals, cost targets and service levels.

Suppliers respond and submit a best price bid for the workload announced. Using the combination of SPY Index score and submitted bid, RampRate select the winning bid using this new frictionless marketplace for more effective transactions.
